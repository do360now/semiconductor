Solid State

The development of reliable semiconductor-based electronic devices has impacted all our lives.

Today, we take for granted the tiny, powerful, and inexpensive electronic devices that are part of our daily lives.

But it wasn't always this way.

Before the 1950s, electronic devices were built using vacuum tubes.

These tubes were large, fragile, and consumed a lot of power.

They were also prone to failure.

Transistors changed all that.

They were the first solid-state electronic devices.

They were small, reliable, and consumed very little power.

They were the foundation of the modern electronics industry.

Transistors were the first step in the development of the integrated circuit.

Integrated circuits are the building blocks of all modern electronic devices.

They are the reason why electronic devices are so small, powerful, and inexpensive.

They are the reason why we have the Internet, smartphones, and so much more.

But it all started with the transistor.

By the 1950s, vacuum tube electronics had reached a plateau.

New, more reliable components were needed.

In 1947 scientists at the Bell Laboratories demonstrated a solid state amplifier built from germanium.

Their device, the transistor, could perform the same functions as a vacuum tube, but it was very small, offered increased reliability, and required low power.

The 1950s was the age of the transistor.
