INTRODUCTION

Solid State

The development of reliable semiconductor-based electronic devices has impacted all our lives. By the 1950s, vacuum tube electronics had reached a plateau. New, more reliable components were needed. In 1947 scientists at the Bell Laboratories demonstrated a solid state amplifier built from germanium. Their device, the transistor, could perform the same functions as a vacuum tube, but it was very small, offered increased reliability, and required low power. The 1950s was the age of the transistor.
